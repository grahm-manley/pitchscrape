Dedication 6

  • Young Money Entertainment
\xe2\x80\xa2 2017
5.8
  • by Sheldon Pearce
    Contributing Writer
  • Rap
January 4 2018

On the first Dedication mixtape installment in four years, Lil Wayne and DJ Drama show some of their former spark, but the tape does little to course-correct Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s spiraling career trajectory.

\n

The earliest installments in the Dedication mixtape series have a special place in internet mixtape mythology. The first was a prototype, the second is among the greatest mixtapes of all time, and each one since has helped to cement Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s legacy as an imaginative and unstoppable rap force. Few rappers have made mixtapes more essential to their reputations and discographies, and these not only defined an era but helped to rewrite the rulebook on how to become a superstar in a digital age. (No wonder Chance the Rapper often cites the New Orleans legend as his \xe2\x80\x9cbiggest inspiration.\xe2\x80\x9d)

\n

The tapes revealed a prolific artist at the peak of his powers paired with a DJ that knew how to edit him. \xe2\x80\x9cWeezy and Dram\xe2\x80\x99\xe2\x80\x94We are the Mixtape Blueprint,\xe2\x80\x9d Drama shouts in the opening seconds of Dedication 6, reminding listeners of their pedigree. Dedication mixtapes are always constructed in the same way: furious, back-to-back one-liners rapped over the beats of the moment\xe2\x80\x94in this case Lil Uzi Vert\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cXO Tour Llif3,\xe2\x80\x9d 21 Savage\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cBank Account,\xe2\x80\x9d JAY-Z\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cThe Story of O.J.,\xe2\x80\x9d Kendrick Lamar\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cDNA.,\xe2\x80\x9d and more\xe2\x80\x94a rap gauntlet designed to challenge him and measure his raps against those of his most popular peers. In his prime, Lil Wayne would completely reclaim a beat as his own and there was a sense of ease to the process, like elaborate thoughts were just pouring out of him as though he could go on forever.

\n

The first installment in four years, Dedication 6 is a calculated return to a recognizable brand during the most tumultuous period in Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s storied, two-decade career, both artistically and financially. In this time of turmoil, he returns to the well, mostly rapping about Wraiths, jump-offs, and codeine as a numbing agent. The process seems less like it could go on forever and more like it\xe2\x80\x99s merely running on a loop. Wayne got a lot of mileage out of his raps and isn\xe2\x80\x99t all out of tricks just yet, but he\xe2\x80\x99s slowing. \xe2\x80\x9cI been walkin\xe2\x80\x99 on this fuckin\xe2\x80\x99 water for a long time,\xe2\x80\x9d he raps on the highlight \xe2\x80\x9cXO Tour Life,\xe2\x80\x9d pointing to a decade of otherworldly performances. Even he seems to recognize the pace is unsustainable. This is a different Wayne than the one who seemed to unspool endless, enchanting yarns, who had a panoramic understanding of both pop culture and street culture. This Dedication does little to course-correct Lil Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s spiraling career trajectory.

\n

Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s promising run of 2016 features\xe2\x80\x94from Solange\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cMad\xe2\x80\x9d to Chance\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cNo Problem\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x94showed he was still capable. His rapping was less intuitive and his non sequiturs could be grating, but he still has the same instincts. Sometimes muscle memory kicks in and he delivers a devastating string of bars, something as vivid as, \xe2\x80\x9cThis is that codeine overkill/My mud colder than Soldier Field,\xe2\x80\x9d or a new angle like, \xe2\x80\x9cYou never been in jail, I never been in a Corolla/Then I roll a blunt \xe2\x80\x98bout as thick as a Samoan.\xe2\x80\x9d But for every punch as delightfully unorthodox as \xe2\x80\x9cBoyz 2 Menace\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cKush loud as Fred screamin\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98Wilma!\xe2\x80\x99 nigga,\xe2\x80\x9d there are a handful that are too easy to anticipate or that just don\xe2\x80\x99t connect.

\n

Spontaneity used to be a driving force in Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s world, but the punchlines on Dedication 6 are mechanical and the ideas within are rudimentary or sometimes incomplete. He uncorks some of the most eyebrow-raising conceits on nearly the entire first verse of \xe2\x80\x9cFly Away\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x94performed over Kendrick\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cDNA.\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x94as a play on acronyms and letters, fumbling basic premises: Easy (Eazy) like NWA, treating beef like USDA, beating foes up like MMA or getting felt up like TSA. The set-up telegraphs the punch. There are many couplets as stale and stupid as \xe2\x80\x9cI got two keys of that Bieber/Call \xe2\x80\x98em Justin and Justina.\xe2\x80\x9d The only real surprise is that his embarrassing raps can still find new, unexplored levels of embarrassment.

\n

Wayne was once the master of the \xe2\x80\x9cso clever it\xe2\x80\x99s hard to believe no one has rapped it before\xe2\x80\x9d bar, and there are still some of those mixed in (\xe2\x80\x9cBullet showers lead to bloodbaths,\xe2\x80\x9d or, \xe2\x80\x9cTreatin\xe2\x80\x99 medication scrips like some Revelation scripts\xe2\x80\x9d). But there\xe2\x80\x99s a thin line between the eureka moment of perfectly articulating a concise turn of phrase and just artlessly heaving jokes against a wall. Wayne gets in trouble attempting to connect every idea that pops into his head in this way; when every bar has to be a clever quip, you\xe2\x80\x99re bound to mass-produce clunkers. He ends up with reaches like, \xe2\x80\x9cI rob his ass like Robin Givens,\xe2\x80\x9d and corny parallels like, \xe2\x80\x9cMy trashiest hoes clean as fuck.\xe2\x80\x9d No song is without several of these bloopers, most songs are inundated by them, and while the tape is more or less a Christmas Day gift for fans, these sequences shouldn\xe2\x80\x99t constantly derail the momentum, especially when the Wayne mixtape blueprint has always been about fluidity and his breathtaking, death-defying stream-of-consciousness.

\n

The raps themselves are hit or miss, but Lil Wayne is still rapping like a man seeking freedom on Dedication 6, fighting to reestablish a dialogue with listeners and to escape from label purgatory. (The mixtape was released exclusively on DatPiff and withheld from streaming, likely because of Wayne\xe2\x80\x99s ongoing battle with Cash Money over his right to make money off his music without the label.) Given all he\xe2\x80\x99s been through and all he\xe2\x80\x99s done, it\xe2\x80\x99s hard not to root for him, and the verses do track better here than on recent projects. He\xe2\x80\x99s locked in on \xe2\x80\x9cNew Freezer,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cMy Dawgs\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cBlackin Out,\xe2\x80\x9d where whiny flows either explode into shrieks or dissolve into mumbles. The main problem is simply this: He\xe2\x80\x99s run out of interesting ways to say he\xe2\x80\x99s drugged up and horny. The same patented mixtape formula that made him a star and luminary exposes the limitations of a now one-dimensional method that\'s wearing thin.
